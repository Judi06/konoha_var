Data Protection

1. Introduction

Perrin & Partners is committed to protecting the privacy and personal data of its clients and visitors to its website. This data protection policy aims to inform you about how we collect, use, and protect your personal data in accordance with the General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR) and applicable French legislation.

By using our website or entrusting us with your personal data, you accept the practices described in this policy.

3. Data Collected

We may collect the following types of personal data:

  • Identification data: name, surname, postal address, email address, phone number
  • Professional data: profession, company, position
  • Data related to your legal case: information necessary for processing your matter
  • Navigation data: IP address, cookies, browsing history on our site

4. Purposes of Processing

We process your personal data for the following purposes:

  • Providing our legal and notarial services
  • Managing client relationships and case follow-up
  • Responding to your information requests
  • Sending you communications related to our services (with your consent)
  • Improving our website and services
  • Complying with our legal and regulatory obligations

5. Legal Basis for Processing

We process your personal data on the following legal bases:

  • Performance of a contract: when processing is necessary for the performance of a contract to which you are a party or for the implementation of pre-contractual measures taken at your request
  • Legal obligation: when processing is necessary to comply with a legal obligation to which we are subject
  • Legitimate interest: when processing is necessary for the purposes of the legitimate interests we pursue, provided that your interests or fundamental rights and freedoms do not override these
  • Consent: when you have consented to the processing of your personal data for one or more specific purposes

6. Recipients of Data

Your personal data may be communicated to the following categories of recipients:

  • Members of our firm involved in processing your case
  • Our technical subcontractors (hosting, IT maintenance, etc.)
  • Judicial or administrative authorities when required by law
  • Legal auxiliaries (bailiffs, experts, etc.) necessary for processing your case

We do not sell or rent your personal data to third parties for commercial purposes.

7. Data Retention Period

We retain your personal data only for the time necessary to fulfill the purposes for which they were collected, in compliance with legal prescription periods and legal retention obligations.

  • Client data: for the duration of the contractual relationship and up to 5 years after its end (common law limitation period)
  • Prospect data: up to 3 years from the last contact
  • Navigation data: up to 13 months for certain cookies

8. Data Security

We implement appropriate technical and organizational measures to protect your personal data against accidental or unlawful destruction, accidental loss, alteration, unauthorized disclosure or access.

These measures include:

  • Securing access to our premises and information systems
  • Encryption of sensitive data
  • Raising awareness among our staff about confidentiality requirements
  • Implementing data backup and recovery procedures

9. Your Rights

In accordance with the GDPR and applicable French legislation, you have the following rights regarding your personal data:

  • Right of access: you can obtain confirmation that your personal data are being processed and obtain a copy of this data
  • Right to rectification: you can request the correction of inaccurate or incomplete data
  • Right to erasure: you can request the erasure of your data in certain circumstances
  • Right to restriction of processing: you can request the limitation of processing of your data in certain circumstances
  • Right to data portability: you can receive your data in a structured format and transmit it to another controller
  • Right to object: you can object to the processing of your data in certain circumstances
  • Right to withdraw your consent: when processing is based on your consent, you can withdraw it at any time
  • Right to define guidelines regarding the fate of your data after your death

To exercise these rights, you can contact us by email at contact@perrin-associes.fr or by mail at the following address: Perrin & Partners, 15 Place Grangier, 21000 Dijon, France.

We will endeavor to respond to your request within one month of receipt. This period may be extended by two additional months if necessary, taking into account the complexity and number of requests.

11. Cookies

Our website uses cookies to improve your browsing experience. Cookies are small text files stored on your device that allow us to recognize your browser and offer you certain features.

We use the following types of cookies:

  • Technical cookies: necessary for the proper functioning of the site
  • Analytical cookies: allow us to measure the site's audience and analyze your navigation

You can configure your browser to refuse all cookies or to be warned when a cookie is sent. However, some features of the site may not function properly if cookies are disabled.
