Legal Framework

1. Legal Framework of Our Firm

Perrin & Partners is a law and notary firm subject to the rules and ethical principles of the legal and notarial professions in France. Our firm conducts its activities in strict compliance with applicable legal and regulatory provisions, including:

  • Law No. 71-1130 of December 31, 1971, on the reform of certain judicial and legal professions
  • Decree No. 91-1197 of November 27, 1991, organizing the legal profession
  • Ordinance No. 45-2590 of November 2, 1945, on the status of notaries
  • Decree No. 45-0117 of December 19, 1945, implementing the notarial status
  • The National Internal Regulations (RIN) of the legal profession
  • The Code of Ethics for notaries

3. Ethical Principles

Our firm adheres to the fundamental ethical principles governing the legal and notarial professions, including:

3.1 Independence

We perform our duties with complete independence, free from any external pressure, whether economic, political, or social. This independence is essential to ensure the quality and impartiality of our advice and actions.

3.2 Professional Secrecy

We are bound by professional secrecy, in accordance with the provisions of Article 226-13 of the Penal Code. This secrecy covers all information and documents entrusted to us by our clients as part of our mission. It is absolute, general, and unlimited in time.

3.3 Confidentiality

Beyond legal professional secrecy, we are committed to maintaining the strictest confidentiality regarding our clients' affairs, including information not covered by professional secrecy.

3.4 Competence

We are committed to maintaining a high level of competence in our areas of expertise through continuous training and ongoing legal monitoring. We only accept cases for which we have the necessary skills.

3.5 Loyalty

We act with loyalty, both towards our clients and towards the courts, administrations, and colleagues. This loyalty implies, in particular, the absence of conflicts of interest and respect for the rules of professional courtesy.

3.6 Diligence

We handle the cases entrusted to us with diligence, respecting deadlines and keeping our clients regularly informed of the progress of their case.

4. Conflict of Interest Prevention

Our firm has implemented strict procedures to prevent conflicts of interest:

  • Systematic verification of the existence of a potential conflict of interest before accepting a new case
  • Maintenance of a register of clients and opposing parties
  • Implementation of "Chinese walls" when necessary and possible
  • Immediate information to the client in case of detection of a conflict of interest
  • Refusal or termination of the mission in case of proven conflict of interest

5. Fees and Billing

In accordance with legal and ethical provisions, our fees are set according to the following criteria:

  • The complexity of the case
  • The time devoted to the case
  • The experience and specialization of the professional in charge of the case
  • The importance of the interests at stake
  • The financial situation of the client
  • The expenses incurred

For notarial services, our fees are set in accordance with the regulated notary fee schedule, defined by decree.

For legal services, a written fee agreement is systematically established for any new mission, in accordance with Article 10 of the Law of December 31, 1971.

Our invoices detail precisely the services provided and the expenses incurred.

7. Continuing Education

In accordance with our professional obligations, all members of our firm regularly undergo training to maintain and develop their skills:

  • The attorneys in our firm comply with the continuing education obligation of 20 hours per year, in accordance with Article 14-2 of the Law of December 31, 1971
  • The notaries in our firm comply with the continuing education obligation of 30 hours over a period of 3 years, in accordance with Decree No. 2011-1230 of September 30, 2011

Beyond these legal obligations, our firm strongly encourages participation in conferences, seminars, and specialized training in our areas of expertise.

8. Complaint Handling

Our firm has implemented a complaint handling procedure aimed at quickly and effectively resolving any dispute that may arise with a client:

  1. Any complaint must be addressed in writing to the partner in charge of the case or to the head of the firm
  2. An acknowledgment of receipt is sent within 48 hours
  3. The complaint is examined within a maximum period of 15 days
  4. A written and reasoned response is sent to the client

In case of persistent disagreement, the client may refer the matter to:

  • For legal services: the President of the Dijon Bar Association
  • For notarial services: the Chamber of Notaries of Côte-d'Or

9. Anti-Money Laundering and Counter-Terrorism Financing

As legal professionals, we are subject to the vigilance and reporting obligations provided for by the Monetary and Financial Code in the fight against money laundering and terrorist financing.

In this context, we are required to:

  • Identify our clients and, where applicable, the beneficial owners of operations
  • Gather information on the purpose and nature of the business relationship
  • Exercise constant vigilance over the business relationship
  • Retain documents relating to client identity and operations performed

These obligations apply with respect for professional secrecy, in accordance with the legal provisions specific to our professions.
